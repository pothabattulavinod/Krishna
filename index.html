Krishna Janmashtami: Overview

Krishna Janmashtami is celebrated on the eighth day (Ashtami) of the dark fortnight (Krishna Paksha) in the month of Bhadrapada, which usually falls in August or September. The festival commemorates the birth of Lord Krishna, the eighth avatar of Vishnu, marking his arrival on earth to restore cosmic order and eradicate evil.

Key Celebrations and Rituals

Devotees celebrate Krishna Janmashtami with fasting, singing devotional songs, and performing plays reenacting episodes from Krishna's life. Temples and homes are decorated with flowers, lights, and traditional items. The festival culminates with the midnight worship, which is believed to be the time of Krishna's birth.

Krishna's Birthplace

Krishna was born in Mathura, a city in Uttar Pradesh, India. The birthplace is now a major pilgrimage site with the Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple dedicated to him.

Lord Krishna's Iconography

Krishna is often depicted with a flute (bansuri) and a peacock feather in his crown, symbolizing his divine attributes. The flute signifies his enchanting music, while the peacock feather represents beauty and grace.

Significance of Love

Lord Krishna's life is a beautiful portrayal of love and devotion. His relationship with Radha is symbolic of divine love that transcends all boundaries. It teaches us about the essence of pure, selfless love and devotion.

Protection of Cows

Krishna is also known as the protector of cows and a beloved friend to the pastoral community. His childhood spent in the company of cows is celebrated with great reverence, highlighting his role in nurturing and caring for all living beings.
